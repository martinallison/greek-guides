So... is Modern Greek the same as Ancient Greek?

Nope. Modern Greek has much easier pronunciation and grammar. The two look similar when written down, but Greeks normally can’t understand much without studying it.

It’s easier? But the letters still look different

Yeah, Greek doesn’t use the same alphabet as English (the Latin alphabet): it uses the ...Greek one:

Some of them don’t look that different. On the other hand there’s stuff like ψ and ξ which can surely only exist to confuse and baffle.

The good news is that, unlike English, there are no hidden sounds, silent Ks or other nonsense. Most letters only have one pronunciation (there are only three exceptions) so once you learn each one you can pronounce pretty much any Greek word!

What does it sound like then?

Since it’s basically unrelated to any other modern languages, Greek has quite a distinctive sound. Greek spoken quickly by a native is an amazing stream of seemingly impossible consonant combinations.

To demonstrate, here are some Greek tongue twisters:

Άσπρη πέτρα ξέξασπρη, κι απ’τον ήλιο ξεξασπρότερη
Bright white rock, and yet brighter from the sun
Μία πάπια, μα ποιά πάπια; Μια πάπια με παπιά
A duck, but which duck? A duck with ducklings
Μια τίγρη με τρία τιγράκια
A tiger with three cubs
