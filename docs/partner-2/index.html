If you’re lucky enough to have found yourself a Greek partner, you might want to be able to say things to them in Greek.

Let’s start big:

Σ’αγαπώ (sa-ga-PO) is short for σε αγαπώ—literally ‘you I love’. You can say this to family members as well as a partner.

It’s rough equivalent in English letters is ’s’agapo’, with the emphasis on the last ‘o’.

Extra points if you put your hands together on your chest and look to the sky like it’s made of puppies while you say it.

Extra extra points if you add πολύ (po-LEE—‘a lot’) to the end and smoosh the words together to make σ’αγαπώπολύυυυυυυυυ (sagapopo-LEEEEEEEEE).

If someone says it to you first, you can say κι εγώ σ’αγαπώ (kye-GO sa-ga-PO) which means ‘I love you too’.

These are kind of opposite ends of the same spectrum.

Αγάπη μου (ag-AP-ee moo) is like ‘darling’ or ‘love’ and what you’d use if you’re being cutesy. It can be used with family members too… in a non-weird way.

Μωρό μου (mo-RO moo) literally means ‘my baby’. You might hear ε μορώ on its own too, from sleazy guys in bars (or the street, even) if you’re an attractive woman.
