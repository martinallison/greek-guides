Let’s start with the easy letters. They’re basically just English ones dressed up in a different skin (though, since the English—or Latin—alphabet comes from Greek, these are actually the originals).

Α • α άλφα alpha

Similar to the ‘a’ in far but more open

English equivalent: a

Ε • ε έψιλον epsilon

Similar to the ‘e’ in ferry but more open

English equivalent: e

Κ • κ κάππα kappa

Like English ‘k’ but without the tiny bit of air at the end

English equivalent: k

Μ • μ μυ my

Exactly like English ‘m’

English equivalent: m

Τ • τ ταυ taf

Like English ’t’ but again with no puff of air at the end

English equivalent: t

Words

With just those letters, you can already say a (slightly random) selection of Greek words:

μα ma but
με me with
μετά meta after
κακά kaka badly
